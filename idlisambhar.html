Idli Sambhar

Idli Sambar is a hearty, satisfying, comforting and a healthy meal of soft fluffy idlis served with savory, spiced and lightly tangy sambar:a vegetable stew made with lentils and assorted vegetables. It is a winning combination made for each other.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

4 servings

Tags : South Indian Breakfast Aromatic Food

instructions

step 1

COOKING OF LENTILS: Rinse dal 3-4 times until the water runs clear.

step 2

Transfer the rinsed dal to an Instant Pot stainless steel insert, or stovetop pressure cooker with 2 cups of water, 1 teaspoon salt. Close the lid.

step 3

When the pressure comes down (pin drops) open the lid. Using a whisk, mash the dal for a smooth texture.

step 4

MAKING OF IDLI SAMBHAR: Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pot, add the mustard seeds and allow them to pop. Then add the cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Saute for a few seconds until the curry leaves crisp up.

step 5

Stir in the onions (I am using some red onion and a few pearl onions), and saute for 1 min. Add the rest of the veggies, carrots, potatoes, eggplant, french beans, and drumsticks. Saute for 2 mins.

step 6

Stir in tomatoes and saute until tomatoes are soft. You can cover and cook them for faster cooking.

step 7

Now add the spices - turmeric, red chili powder, sambar powder, and salt. Saute on medium-low heat for a minute, be careful not to burn the spices. Add 3 cups of water and mix well.Cover, and cook until the veggies are soft/ cooked (approximately 7-10 mins). Stir intermittently if required.

step 8

Strain the soaked tamarind using a sieve into the veggies. Mix well and simmer the sambar for 5 mins.

step 9

Add the cooked mashed dal and water as required to adjust the consistency, add 2 cups. Bring the sambar to a boil and simmer for 2 mins.

step 10

Add jaggery and cilantro. Mix well and simmer for another 5 mins. Hotel-style Tiffin sambar is ready!! Serve hot.
